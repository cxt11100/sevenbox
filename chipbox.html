SevenBox

SevenBox is an online tool for sketching and sharing instrumental music — like BeepBox, with live multiplayer so friends can jam in the same song.

All song data is in your browser. When you play multipplayer, the room keeps everyone in sync. Songs you create are yours — export anytime. Free to use.

Multiplayer

Enter a name (nickname + 3–5 numbers, e.g. jam482) → Join a public room or Create room → Copy invite link for friends. Space play/pause and edits sync for everyone in the room.

Instructions

You can add or remove notes by clicking on the gray rows at the top. SevenBox automatically plays the notes out loud for you. Try it!

Notes go into patterns, and you can edit one pattern at a time. Those numbered boxes at the bottom of the editor are the different patterns you can edit. Click the boxes to move to a different part of the song, or click the arrows on the currently selected box to swap which pattern is played during that part of the song.

SevenBox can play several rows of patterns simultaneously, and each row has its own set of patterns. Most rows can play melodies or harmonies, but the bottom row is for drums.

The purple loop underneath the numbered boxes controls which part of the song is currently repeating. Move the loop to listen to a different part of the song, or drag the ends to expand the loop to include the whole song.

When the editor has focus, you can use these keyboard shortcuts:

  • Spacebar: play or pause the song (syncs in multipplayer)
  • Ctrl/Cmd + F: restart from the start for everyone in the room
  • Z: undo · Y or Shift Z: redo
  • C / V: copy / paste selected pattern(s)
  • 0–9: assign pattern number to selection
  • Arrows: move selection

In the note pattern editor, click and drag horizontally on a note to adjust its duration. Click above or below an existing note to add more notes to a chord.

About

SevenBox is based on the BeepBox editor by John Nesky, with multipplayer rooms added for live jams. Neither SevenBox nor BeepBox claim ownership of songs you create.
